Today's Journaling Prompts 

 

Prompt 1: Wait and Observe: When Emotions and Thoughts Interact

 

Instruction: Based on what you've accumulated this week, let's examine what happens when you hold in your emotions and thoughts.

 

How?

  • Choose one or two events where emotions and thoughts were strongly connected.

    There’s nothing wrong with how you feel. But did you try to control those emotions and thoughts by taking certain actions?

    If you did, consider what would happen if you refrained from taking that action and simply observed the sensation. What would change?

    If you were able to regulate your emotions, what allowed you to do so?

     

Examples:

  • Emotions: Anxiety - At a networking event, I felt anxious while talking to someone with a strong presence. I kept trying to keep the conversation going, which left me exhausted. At one point, I started to asking irrelevant and random questions, and the conversation started to lose direction. I could feel the other person losing focus, glancing at people passing by.

    What if I stopped putting so much effort into the conversation? Could I simply take a breath and maintain my composure? It may seem impossible, but I felt like my energy was being drained by the situation. If I had taken a moment to breathe and kept my energy centered, perhaps I would have had more control over the conversation.

     

  • Thoughts: Intimidating Boss: During a meeting with my manager today, I immediately jumped into the agenda. Based on his demeanor, I felt there was no point in trying to build further conversation. But if I had paused and given us a little space, it might have felt like I was letting him get away with everything.

 

Tips for Journaling

  • No one is looking at your entries - try to be as authentic as you can

  • You don't have to force three items - if there's just one strong emotion or thought, that's perfectly fine!

  • For the third box, you can write about your dream or simply put N/A

  • There's no "right way" to journal - your process is unique to you

  • Write in whatever language or style feels most natural

  • Feel free to mix short and detailed entries based on how you feel

  • You can always come back and add more thoughts later

  • Some days will feel more meaningful than others - that's normal!
