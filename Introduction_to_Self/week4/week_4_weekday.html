Today's Journaling Prompts 

 

Prompt 1: Where Emotions and Thoughts Interact

Instruction: Let's examine how your emotions and thoughts are related to each other. Sometimes, they align, but other times, they might feel completely opposite.

 

How?

  • Focus on a few emotions and thoughts that stand out to you.

  • Similar to how we started, but this time, concentrate on specific emotions and thoughts that feel most relevant to you right now.

  • You don’t have to organize them or draw conclusions unless that comes naturally.
     

Examples:

  • Emotions: Anxiety - When I attended a networking event near my office, I felt a little anxious about whether I was presenting myself well. Some people I spoke with seemed important, while others were simply strangers. I feel comfortable talking to strangers, but I tend to get very nervous in front of people who seem powerful. In those moments, I feel like I don’t know what to say, and it almost feels like I’m a child again—powerless in front of those speaking to me.

     

  • Thoughts: Intimidating Boss: I had another meeting with my manager today, and it’s always awkward—it’s not getting any better. For example, when our meeting starts, the small talk is so uncomfortable that I just want to skip to the actual conversation. He doesn’t seem interested in getting to know us; it feels like he’s only engaging in small talk because it’s socially expected. Even during the meeting, the way he looks at me feels like he’s judging every word I say.

 

Tips for Journaling

  • No one is looking at your entries - try to be as authentic as you can

  • You don't have to force three items - if there's just one strong emotion or thought, that's perfectly fine!

  • For the third box, you can write about your dream or simply put N/A

  • There's no "right way" to journal - your process is unique to you

  • Write in whatever language or style feels most natural

  • Feel free to mix short and detailed entries based on how you feel

  • You can always come back and add more thoughts later

  • Some days will feel more meaningful than others - that's normal!
