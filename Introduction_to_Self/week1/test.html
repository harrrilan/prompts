Introduction to Self

This is a introduction to prompted journaling. Let's practice pondering and find yourself.

 

This week's goal: In our first week, we'll focus on identifying emotions that appear frequently in your life. By tracking both emotions and thoughts, you'll begin to notice patterns in your emotional landscape.

 

 

Today's Journaling Prompts 

 

Three Strong Emotions

Instruction: Take a moment to reflect on your day and write down three distinct emotions you experienced.

 

How?

  • Name the specific emotion (e.g., frustration, joy, anxiety).

  • Describe the situation that triggered it.

  • Maybe add little why you felt that way.

  • If it's hard to describe how you feel, you can use metaphor to describe how was it like.

 

Examples:

  • Relief - Finally submitted the project I've been working on for weeks. Every time there's a deadline, it feels like a car driving too close behind me.

  • My partner made me breakfast before work and I was really grateful. Although we've been having bad communication, this could open up some conversations. Maybe I was a little too stubborn.

     

     

Three Significant thoughts

Instruction: Reflect on the thoughts that stood out today.

 

These could be:

  • Self-observations

  • Recurring ideas

  • Sudden realizations

  • Concerns about the future

  • Reflections on past events

     

How?

  • Write it down exactly as it came to you

  • Note what you were doing when the thought occurred

     

Examples:

  • Am I too quiet when ordering in restaurants? This tends to happen when I spend the entire day by myself.

  • Maybe going back to school for a Master's degree would be a great idea. With all the busyness, I want to give myself some time.

 

 

Tips for Journaling

  • No one is looking at your entries - try to be as authentic as you can

  • You don't have to force three items - if there's just one strong emotion or thought, that's perfectly fine!

  • For the third box, you can write about your dream or simply put N/A

  • There's no "right way" to journal - your process is unique to you

  • Write in whatever language or style feels most natural

  • Feel free to mix short and detailed entries based on how you feel

  • You can always come back and add more thoughts later

  • Some days will feel more meaningful than others - that's normal!
